The Environmental Soil Science Lab at Mississippi State University, led by Dr. Madhav Dhakal, investigates how soil, water, plants, and the atmosphere interact in agricultural systems. Our research spans soil-plant-water relations, evapotranspiration, greenhouse gas emissions, soil health, and climate-smart agriculture, with the goal of building more productive, resource-efficient, and resilient cropping systems.

We combine field experimentation with advanced monitoring and modeling — including eddy covariance flux towers, static gas-flux chambers, soil-water sensing, remote sensing, and process-based simulation — to quantify carbon, water, and energy exchange from the field to the ecosystem scale. Current work focuses on rice and corn production systems across the Mississippi Delta and beyond.
